Complexity & High Dimensions

What happens when we have 3, 4, or 100 attributes?

1. The Visualization Barrier (3D and beyond)

We can visualize 1D lines and 2D planes easily. We can barely visualize 3D space using rotation.

The Blunt Truth: Humans have zero intuition for 4D geometry. We cannot visualize it directly. Professionals use tricks like projections (squashing 4D into 2D) or simpler models to understand it.

Instruction: Click and drag on the chart above to rotate the 3D space.

❓ So how do we handle 4D+ in reality?
  • Projections: Techniques like PCA or t-SNE "squash" high dimensions down to 2D plots so we can see clusters.
  • Feature Importance: Bar charts showing which attributes contributed most to the decision.
  • We don't visualize geometry: We visualize the rules instead. (See below).

2. The Solution: Decision Trees ("The Recursive Cutter")

When geometry gets too complex, we switch strategies. Instead of trying to draw one perfect curvy line, we ask a sequence of simple Yes/No questions to chop up the space.

💡 Memory Anchor: The Staircase / The Boxes

Think of repeated cuts that funnel data into smaller and smaller boxes. The boundary ends up looking like a jagged staircase.
