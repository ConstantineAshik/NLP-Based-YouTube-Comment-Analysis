Theory: Gini vs Entropy

Gini Impurity: Measures the probability of misclassifying a randomly chosen element if it were randomly labeled according to the distribution of labels in the set.
Formula: $1 - \sum p_i^2$

Entropy: Measures the level of disorder or uncertainty. High entropy means equal mix (chaos); low entropy means pure (order).
Formula: $- \sum p_i \log_2(p_i)$

2. Feature Race (Fisher Score)

Which feature separates classes best?
Goal: Maximize Distance between Means / Minimize Variance within Classes.
Theory: Fisher Score

The Fisher Score evaluates how well a feature discriminates between two classes.

$$F = \frac{(\mu_1 - \mu_2)^2}{\sigma_1^2 + \sigma_2^2}$$

  • Numerator: Distance between class centers (we want this huge).
  • Denominator: Spread (variance) within classes (we want this tiny).

Theory: Information Gain

When building Decision Trees, we split data to reduce impurity. The algorithm calculates the Weighted Average Impurity of the children:

$$Cost = \frac{N_{left}}{N_{total}} \times I_{left} + \frac{N_{right}}{N_{total}} \times I_{right}$$

We choose the split that minimizes this cost (or maximizes Information Gain).
