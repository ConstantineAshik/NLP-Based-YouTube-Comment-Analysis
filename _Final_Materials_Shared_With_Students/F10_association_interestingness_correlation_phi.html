Part 4: Judging Patterns (Correlation)

In Part 3, we generated rules. Now, we must evaluate them. High Confidence is not enough!

1. The "Confidence Trap"

Consider a rule: {Cereal} $\to$ {Milk}. If everyone buys Milk anyway, the rule is misleading.

Rule: {A} $\to$ {B} | Confidence: -- | Correlation: --
Observation: Click "Scenario B". Notice that B (Milk) is in almost every basket.
Even though A $\to$ B has High Confidence, A and B are actually Independent (or even negatively correlated!).

2. Statistical Coefficient of Correlation ($\phi$)

We use correlation (similar to Lift) to measure the strength of the connection beyond mere frequency.

Corr: 0.00
The Logic:
Positive (> 0): Buying A increases chance of B. (Complementary goods).
Zero (~ 0): Buying A has no effect on B. (Independent).
Negative (< 0): Buying A decreases chance of B. (Substitute goods, e.g., Tea vs Coffee).
